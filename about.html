Our story

Built on hide, held by thread.

Hamza Shoes started in a small Karachi workshop with one belief: leather footwear should be made the slow way — cut by hand, shaped over wooden lasts, and saddle-stitched so the seam moves with your foot instead of fighting it.

Today we make shoes, sandals, slippers and loafers for men and women, using the same process we started with. No factory shortcuts, no synthetic soles passed off as leather — just real hide, real stitching, and pairs meant to be resoled, not replaced.

What we stand for

Hide

Full-grain and top-grain leather sourced locally — no synthetic blends, ever.

Hand

Every pair is cut and saddle-stitched by an artisan, not stamped out by machine.

Hold

Built to be resoled and repaired — footwear meant to last years, not seasons.

From hide to pair

01

Selecting the hide

We inspect each leather sheet for grain quality and thickness before it's approved for cutting.

02

Hand-cutting

Patterns are cut by hand against the grain direction to keep each panel strong and consistent.

03

Saddle-stitching

Two needles, one thread, locked through every hole — a seam that won't unravel if one stitch breaks.

04

Finishing

Edges are burnished, soles attached, and every pair is hand-inspected before it leaves the workshop.
